# Tythe DAO

Tythe DAO is the decentralized governance and arbitration layer of the Tythe protocol. It ensures that no single entity (including Tythe itself) can unilaterally control scoring parameters, dispute outcomes, or protocol direction.

The DAO operates through two distinct arms, each serving a fundamentally different function.

***

**Justice Arm:** Handles disputed slashes and contested scoring events. AI clerks prepare evidence briefs. Human juries drawn from the same TCT band as the disputant hear and resolve each case. The Justice Arm is the protocol's check against enforcement errors.

**Governance Arm:** Governs scoring parameters, formula upgrades, treasury allocation, and protocol direction via the TYT token. The Governance Arm activates once the TYT token is live and the protocol has established a verifiable data foundation worth governing.

***

The separation is intentional. Justice and governance require different participants, different incentive structures, and different timelines. Combining them at launch would introduce governance complexity before the protocol has the data or the community to govern responsibly.
