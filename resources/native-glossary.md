# Native Glossary

The canonical definitions for every term in the Tythe protocol.

***

#### Protocol

<table><thead><tr><th width="150">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>Tythe Protocol</strong></td><td>The neutral, decentralized infrastructure for credit. Provides the identity (CEP), intelligence (TCT), and integration (CEW) layers required to transform on-chain behavioral data into verifiable, actionable creditworthiness.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TCE Standard</strong></td><td><strong>Tythe Credit Enhancement Standard.</strong> The canonical framework defining how behavioral telemetry and capacity metrics are scored and tokenized into a machine-enforceable financial risk signal. The current live model is TCE-26.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TCE-26</strong></td><td>The protocol's launch scoring model. Defines the formula structure, input weights, band thresholds, and parameter set used to calculate TCT scores. Future models are activated only via a successful Tythe DAO governance proposal.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TYT</strong></td><td><strong>The Tythe protocol token.</strong> Used for Tythe DAO governance, Credit Voucher issuer staking, and protocol incentive alignment. Launches in V1.2.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>CAQB</strong></td><td><strong>Collateral Asset Quality Benchmark.</strong> A risk-weighting index that categorizes collateral assets from AAA to CCC quality tiers based on 180-day realized volatility and secondary market depth. Governs both the Volatility Quotient component of the Reliability Score and the MVV ceiling for a given position.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Tythe DAO</strong></td><td>The decentralized governance and arbitration layer of the protocol. Operates two arms: the Justice Arm (dispute resolution) and the Governance Arm (formula and parameter governance). Activates in V1.2.</td></tr></tbody></table>

#### Identity

<table><thead><tr><th width="235">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CEP</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Enhancement Profile.</strong> The sovereign financial identity layer. Anchored on the <code>did:cheqd</code> network, the CEP is a permanent, privacy-preserving financial identity that unifies a participant's wallets, credentials, attestations, and credit history into one resolvable profile.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>DID-Linked Resource</strong></td><td>Any on-chain resource (wallet addresses, EIP-712 attestations, TCT balance, verifiable credentials) attached directly to a participant's DID. Creates a unified, resolvable financial profile.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Proof of Personhood (PoP)</strong></td><td>An optional biometric-anchored credential (via World ID or equivalent) attached to a CEP. Grants a fixed +5 TCT boost and strengthens Sybil resistance.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>zk-KYC</strong></td><td>ZK identity verification and sanctions screening provided via Billions. Processed entirely via zero-knowledge attestation;<br>no raw identity data is held by the protocol at any point.</td></tr></tbody></table>

#### Intelligence

<table><thead><tr><th width="175">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>TCT</strong></td><td><strong>Tokenized Creditworthiness.</strong> A non-transferable ERC-20 token representing a participant's credit character score (300-850). The primary credit signal used to price risk, apply enhancements, and enforce limits across integrated markets.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>tMVV</strong></td><td><strong>Transaction credit limit.</strong> The maximum value-based enhancement CEW can apply on any single transaction, expressed in USD. A hard ceiling per transaction; enhancements apply up to tMVV, transactions above tMVV proceed at standard terms for the remainder. Derived from Capital Capacity Markers and updated at Refresh.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>rMVV</strong></td><td><strong>30-day rolling credit limit.</strong> The total value-based enhancement budget across all transactions in a 30-day window. rMVV = tMVV × 10. Depletes in real time as enhancements are applied across all integrated markets. Resets automatically every 30 days from first consumption. When rMVV is exhausted, no further value-based enhancements until the window resets.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Staged Score</strong></td><td>A participant's current off-chain creditworthiness, continuously computed by the protocol as behavior changes. Always current. Distinct from the on-chain TCT balance, which updates only on Manual Refresh.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Manual Refresh</strong></td><td>The participant-initiated process of tokenizing their staged score as updated on-chain TCT, tMVV, and rMVV values. Triggered via the Tythe Dashboard. Participant pays gas.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Auto-Slash</strong></td><td>The protocol's automated enforcement mechanism. Upon a confirmed nEvent, the protocol immediately reduces the participant's on-chain TCT balance. Slash severity is determined by the participant's percentile rank within the relevant nEvent label's spectrum. Gasless.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Drops and Buckets</strong></td><td>The asymmetric mechanic governing TCT movement. Positive score changes accumulate gradually in drops; earned through consistent, responsible behavior over time. Negative changes are applied in buckets; swift, significant reductions triggered by nEvents. <br>TCT is slow to gain, fast to lose.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>nEvent</strong></td><td>Negative Event. A verifiable on-chain credit deterioration (Liquidation, Default, Exploit, Mercenary, Informed, or Whale) detected, classified, and ranked by the Relay Emitter. Where applicable, nEvents trigger an automated enforcement response.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Relay Emitter</strong></td><td>The off-chain ML-powered intelligence layer built on gradient boosting models (XGBoost and LightGBM). Continuously monitors on-chain behavioral patterns, classifies negative credit events, and ranks each participant within their label's spectrum by severity. Percentile rank determines Auto-Slash severity. Ranked nEvent labels are broadcast to institutional subscribers and consumed internally by the deterministic scoring engine.</td></tr></tbody></table>

#### Integration

<table><thead><tr><th width="160">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CEW</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Enhancement Wrapper.</strong> A smart contract proxy that intercepts financial transactions in real time and applies TCT-based enhancements, enforcement, or blocks automatically. Value-based enhancements are capped at the lower of tMVV and available rMVV. Rate-based enhancements are governed by TCT bracket only with no MVV cap.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Enhancement</strong></td><td>The CEW action applied to Excellent and Very Good bracket participants. Delivers upgraded capital terms (reduced collateral, lower rates, better fees) up to the participant's tMVV and rMVV limits. Every value-based Enhancement depletes the participant's rMVV by the enhancement amount.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Enforcement</strong></td><td>The CEW action applied to Fair and Poor bracket participants. Applies tighter capital terms proportional to the participant's bracket. Does not deplete rMVV.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Blocker</strong></td><td>The CEW action applied to Flagged (score 0) participants when a market has enforcement enabled. Transaction is returned as blocked — the integrated market handles the revert in its own execution logic.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>CEW Fee Switch</strong></td><td>CEW launches fee-free. When activated by the Tythe DAO, a flat 0.07% applies to all three action types. Enhancement fee paid by the participant on-chain at transaction time, Enforcement and Blocker fees tracked off-chain and invoiced monthly to the integrated market.</td></tr></tbody></table>

#### Data

<table><thead><tr><th width="180">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CDX</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Data Exchange.</strong> A module enabling consent-gated, order-specific sale of verified credit data from individuals to institutional buyers. Tythe compiles and delivers reports at minimum fill. <em>(V2)</em></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Dispute Settlement</strong></td><td>The decentralized arbitration process handled by the Tythe DAO Justice Arm. Participants stake TCT to raise a dispute. AI Clerks prepare evidence briefs from on-chain data. A human jury drawn from the disputant's TCT band delivers the verdict. Stake is burned if the slash is upheld, returned in full if overturned.</td></tr></tbody></table>
