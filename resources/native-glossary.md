# Native Glossary

This glossary serves as the canonical taxonomy for the Tythe Credit Standard. It defines the foundational primitives and mechanics that transform raw behavioral signals into verifiable creditworthiness.

***

| Term                            | Definition                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tythe Protocol**              | The neutral, decentralized infrastructure for credit. Tythe provides the identity (CEP), intelligence (TCT), and integration (CEW) layers required to turn raw behavioral data into verifiable creditworthiness and actionable financial reputation.                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **TCE Standard**                | Tythe Credit Enhancement standard. The canonical framework defining how behavioral telemetry, institutional vouches, and capacity metrics are scored and tokenized into a machine-enforceable financial risk signal. The current live model is TCE-26.                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| **TCE-26**                      | The protocol's launch scoring model. Defines the formula structure, input weights, band thresholds, and parameter set used to calculate TCT scores at launch. Future models (TCE-27 and beyond) are activated only via a successful Tythe DAO governance proposal.                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| **TCT**                         | Tokenized Creditworthiness. The primary credit signal. A non-transferable ERC-20 token representing a participant's reliability score (300–850). TCT acts as the live credit intelligence used to price risk, apply discounts, and enforce limits across integrated markets.                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **MVV**                         | Maximum Vouchsafed Value. Metadata embedded within the TCT token defining the maximum dollar value of a position the protocol is willing to vouchsafe for a given participant. Derived from Capital Capacity metrics. A CEW boost is never applied beyond a participant's verified MVV.                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **CEP**                         | Credit Enhancement Profile. The self-sovereign identity layer. Anchored on the `did:cheqd` network and secured by ZK-proofs, the CEP links multiple wallets and off-chain data to a single, privacy-preserving root identity. Everything in the protocol flows through the CEP.                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **CEW**                         | Credit Enhancement Wrapper. The integration layer. A smart contract proxy that intercepts financial transactions in real time to apply TCT-based rate discounts, limit boosts, or risk enforcement logic. The CEW operates within the participant's verified MVV at all times.                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **CEV**                         | Credit Enhancement Vault. The liquidity layer _(V2)_. ERC-4626 vaults where LPs provide collateral top-offs to back TCT-verified borrowers in exchange for yield and risk premiums. CEVs are the only module enabling undercollateralized lending on Tythe — risk is distributed across LPs who opt in, never transferred to institutions.                                                                                                                 |
| **CAVB**                        | Collateral Asset Volatility Benchmark. A risk-weighting index that categorizes collateral assets from AAA (Pristine) to CCC (Junk) based on 180-day realized volatility and secondary market depth. The CAVB governs both the Volatility Quotient component of the Reliability Score and the MVV ceiling for a given position.                                                                                                                             |
| **nEvent**                      | Negative Event. A verifiable on-chain credit deterioration — such as a Liquidation, Default, or Exploit — detected and classified by the Relay Emitter. nEvents trigger an automated enforcement response, including Auto-Slash where applicable.                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| **Auto-Slash**                  | The protocol's proactive enforcement mechanism. Upon a confirmed nEvent, the protocol immediately burns a portion of the participant's TCT balance on-chain, signaling an instantaneous shift in their risk profile. Auto-Slashes are gasless — enforcement is protocol-side.                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Staged Score**                | A participant's current off-chain creditworthiness — continuously updated by the protocol as behavior changes. The staged score exists in a ready-to-attest state and is distinct from the on-chain TCT balance, which is only updated when the participant chooses to Refresh.                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Manual Refresh**              | The participant-initiated process of tokenizing their staged score onto the blockchain as an updated TCT balance. Triggered via the Tythe Dashboard. The participant pays the associated gas costs. Tythe recommends refreshing before opening a new credit position or when a significant score increase has been notified.                                                                                                                               |
| **Drops and Buckets**           | The asymmetric mechanic governing TCT movement. Positive score changes accumulate gradually in drops — earned through consistent, responsible on-chain behavior over time. Negative score changes are applied in buckets — swift, significant reductions triggered by nEvents. This mirrors how trust actually works: slow to build, fast to lose.                                                                                                         |
| **Relay Emitter**               | The AI-powered intelligence layer. The Relay Emitter continuously monitors on-chain behavioral patterns, classifies negative credit events, generates structured nEvent labels, and broadcasts real-time risk-alpha to institutional subscribers. The Relay Emitter informs and labels — scoring is handled separately by the deterministic TCE formula.                                                                                                   |
| **Credit Voucher**              | A signed EIP-712 on-chain attestation issued by a verified institution — bank, fintech, fund, merchant, or AI network — vouching for a participant based on internal due diligence or performance logs. Vouching institutions stake TYT protocol tokens, creating direct accountability for voucher accuracy. _(V1.2)_                                                                                                                                     |
| **Voucher Score**               | The institutional reputation metric assigned to Credit Voucher issuers. Determined by the accuracy and reliability of their historical vouches. Revenue tiers for institutional Relay Emitter access are based on Voucher Score. Inaccurate vouches reduce the issuer's Voucher Score and result in TYT stake penalties.                                                                                                                                   |
| **Dispute Settlement**          | The decentralized arbitration layer of the Tythe DAO Justice Arm. To raise a dispute, participants stake a protocol-defined amount of TCT as a good faith deposit. If the Justice Arm rules the slash was valid, a portion of the staked TCT is burned. If the dispute is upheld, the full stake is returned. AI Clerks prepare evidence briefs from on-chain data; human juries drawn from the same TCT band as the disputant hear and resolve each case. |
| **AI Clerks**                   | An off-chain AI module that parses protocol telemetry and on-chain data to generate structured evidence briefs for the Justice Arm. AI Clerks accelerate dispute resolution without removing human oversight — juries make the final ruling.                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Signature Fee**               | The fee paid to a Credit Voucher issuer whenever their attestation is successfully utilized to back a participant's position. Part of the revenue model incentivizing institutional participation in the Credit Voucher module.                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Enhancement Fee**             | The fee paid by a participant to CEV Liquidity Providers in exchange for collateral top-off services — effectively capital efficiency as a service. _(V2)_                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| **Credit Data Licensing (CDL)** | A V2 module allowing participants to anonymize and license their credit data to institutional researchers, model builders, and AI engineers — and receive direct compensation for it. Tythe takes a platform cut. The participant keeps the rest. _(V2)_                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| **Proof of Personhood (PoP)**   | An optional biometric-anchored credential — via World ID or equivalent protocol — that participants can attach to their CEP. Attaching a verified PoP credential grants a fixed +5 TCT boost and strengthens the profile's Sybil resistance.                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **Trovebook**                   | A participant's personal off-chain credit bureau. A private, encrypted ledger of credit history across DeFi and RWA protocols, manually maintained by the participant. Trovebook entries contribute to the CEP's data richness and support stronger scoring inputs. Trovebook will evolve into a fully self-custodial on-chain bureau in a future release.                                                                                                 |
| **TYT**                         | The Tythe protocol token. Used for Tythe DAO governance participation, Credit Voucher issuer staking, and protocol incentive alignment. Launches in V1.2 alongside the Governance Arm and Credit Voucher module.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
