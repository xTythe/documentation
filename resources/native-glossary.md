# Native Glossary

The canonical definitions for every term in the Tythe protocol.

***

### Protocol

<table><thead><tr><th width="150">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>Tythe Protocol</strong></td><td>The neutral, decentralized infrastructure for credit. Provides the identity (CEP), intelligence (TCT), and integration (CEW) layers required to transform on-chain behavioral data into verifiable, actionable creditworthiness.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TCE Standard</strong></td><td>Tythe Credit Enhancement Standard. The canonical framework defining how behavioral telemetry and capacity metrics are scored and tokenized into a machine-enforceable financial risk signal. The current live model is TCE-26.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TCE-26</strong></td><td>The protocol's launch scoring model. Defines the formula structure, input weights, band thresholds, and parameter set used to calculate TCT scores. Future models are activated only via a successful Tythe DAO governance proposal.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TYT</strong></td><td>The Tythe protocol token. Used for Tythe DAO governance, Credit Voucher issuer staking, and protocol incentive alignment. Launches in V1.2.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>CAVB</strong></td><td>Collateral Asset Volatility Benchmark. A risk-weighting index that categorizes collateral assets from AAA (Pristine) to CCC (Junk) based on 180-day realized volatility and secondary market depth. Governs both the Volatility Quotient component of the Reliability Score and the MVV ceiling for a given position.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Tythe DAO</strong></td><td>The decentralized governance and arbitration layer of the protocol. Operates two arms: the Justice Arm (dispute resolution) and the Governance Arm (formula and parameter governance). Activates in V1.2.</td></tr></tbody></table>

### Identity

<table><thead><tr><th width="235">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CEP</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Enhancement Profile.</strong> The sovereign financial identity layer. Anchored on the <code>did:cheqd</code> network, the CEP is a permanent, privacy-preserving financial identity that unifies a participant's wallets, credentials, attestations, and credit history into one resolvable profile.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>DID-Linked Resource</strong></td><td>Any on-chain resource (wallet addresses, EIP-712 attestations, TCT balance, verifiable credentials) attached directly to a participant's DID. Creates a unified, resolvable financial profile.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Proof of Personhood (PoP)</strong></td><td>An optional biometric-anchored credential (via World ID or equivalent) attached to a CEP. Grants a fixed +5 TCT boost and strengthens Sybil resistance.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>zk-KYC</strong></td><td>ZK identity verification and sanctions screening provided via Billions. Processed entirely via zero-knowledge attestation;<br>no raw identity data is held by the protocol at any point.</td></tr></tbody></table>

### Intelligence

<table><thead><tr><th width="175">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>TCT</strong></td><td>Tokenized Creditworthiness. A non-transferable ERC-20 token representing a participant's reliability score (300–850). The primary credit signal used to price risk, apply enhancements, and enforce limits across integrated markets.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>MVV</strong></td><td><strong>Maximum Vouchsafed Value.</strong> The protocol's per-transaction enhancement ceiling, embedded in the TCT token metadata. Enhancements apply up to MVV (txs above MVV proceed at standard terms for the remainder). Derived from Capital Capacity metrics.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Staged Score</strong></td><td>A participant's current off-chain creditworthiness, continuously computed by the protocol as behavior changes. Always current. Distinct from the on-chain TCT balance, which updates only on Manual Refresh.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Manual Refresh</strong></td><td>The participant-initiated process of tokenizing their staged score as an updated on-chain TCT balance and MVV. Triggered via the Tythe Dashboard. Participant pays gas. Refresh before opening a new position or when a significant score increase has been notified.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Auto-Slash</strong></td><td>The protocol's automated enforcement mechanism. Upon a confirmed nEvent, the protocol immediately reduces the participant's on-chain TCT balance. Slash severity is determined by the participant's percentile rank within the relevant nEvent label's spectrum. Gasless enforcement.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Drops and Buckets</strong></td><td>The asymmetric mechanic governing TCT movement. Positive score changes accumulate gradually in drops (earned through consistent, responsible behavior over time). Negative changes are applied in buckets (swift, significant reductions triggered by nEvents). <br><em>TCT is slow to gain, fast to lose.</em></td></tr><tr><td><strong>nEvent</strong></td><td><strong>Negative Event.</strong> A verifiable on-chain credit deterioration (<em>Liquidation</em>, <em>Default</em>, <em>Exploit</em>, <em>Mercenary</em>, <em>Informed</em>, or <em>Whale</em>) detected, classified, and ranked by the Relay Emitter. Where applicable, nEvents trigger an automated enforcement response.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Relay Emitter</strong></td><td>The off-chain ML-powered intelligence layer built on gradient boosting models (XGBoost and LightGBM). Continuously monitors on-chain behavioral patterns, classifies negative credit events, and ranks each participant within their label's spectrum by severity. Percentile rank determines Auto-Slash severity.</td></tr></tbody></table>

### Integration

<table><thead><tr><th width="165">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CEW</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Enhancement Wrapper.</strong> A smart contract proxy that intercepts financial transactions in real time and applies TCT-based enhancements, enforcement, or blocks automatically.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Enhancement</strong></td><td>The CEW action applied to Excellent and Very Good bracket participants. Delivers upgraded capital terms up to the participant's MVV.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Enforcement</strong></td><td>The CEW action applied to Fair and Poor bracket participants. <br>Applies tighter capital terms proportional to the participant's bracket.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Blocker</strong></td><td>The CEW action applied to Flagged (score 0) participants when a market has enforcement enabled. Transaction is returned as blocked while the integrated market handles the revert in its own execution logic.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>CEW Fee Switch</strong></td><td>CEW launches fee-free. When activated by the Tythe DAO, a flat 0.07% (<mark style="color:red;"><code>CEW_FEE_BPS</code></mark>) applies to all three action types. Enhancement fee paid by the participant on-chain at transaction time, Enforcement and Blocker fees tracked off-chain and invoiced monthly to the integrated market.</td></tr></tbody></table>

### Liquidity

<table><thead><tr><th width="150">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CEV</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Enhancement Vault.</strong> ERC-4626 vaults where LPs provide collateral top-offs to back TCT-verified borrowers in exchange for Risk Premium share, float income, and TYT rewards. The only module enabling undercollateralized lending on Tythe. Risk is distributed across LPs who opt in explicitly. <em>(V2)</em></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Public CEV</strong></td><td>A CEV access policy where any CEP-verified participant meeting the vault's TCT Score Minimum and MVV requirements is eligible to borrow. First come first served.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Private CEV</strong></td><td>A CEV access policy where the curator defines a whitelist of eligible borrowers at deployment. TCT Score Minimum set to optional (eligibility can be determined entirely by whitelist membership alone). Curator manages the whitelist via the Dashboard post-deployment.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>TFD</strong></td><td><strong>Total Fixed Deposit.</strong> The vault's total capital capacity threshold. The vault remains inactive and closed to borrowers until TFD is reached for the first time. Once activated, the vault is permanently active regardless of subsequent LP withdrawals.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>RCR</strong></td><td><strong>Risk Concentration Ratio.</strong> The maximum active top-off exposure as a percentage of TFD. Immutable per vault. Ensures a liquidity buffer is always maintained for LP withdrawals and default coverage.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Risk Premium</strong></td><td>The percentage of each top-off amount paid by the borrower at position opening. Set by the curator within DAO-governed floor and ceiling bounds. Tythe retains 9% of every Risk Premium collected as a protocol fee when the fee switch is active.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Float</strong></td><td>Collected Risk Premiums deployed by the vault's allocator into DAO-whitelisted yield strategies. Generates float income for LPs. Managed by the Allocator role and subject to Sentinel oversight.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Curator</strong></td><td>The vault deployer. Sets vault parameters at deployment, manages float deployment, and maintains the borrower whitelist on Private vaults. Parameter changes post-deployment require a timelock.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Sentinel</strong></td><td>A risk-reduction-only role within a CEV. Can immediately recall float, revoke pending timelock actions, and remove yield strategies. Cannot increase risk, move LP capital, or close borrower positions.</td></tr></tbody></table>

### Data

<table><thead><tr><th width="180">Term</th><th>Definition</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>CDX</strong></td><td><strong>Credit Data Exchange.</strong> A module enabling consent-gated, order-specific sale of verified credit data from individuals to institutional buyers. Tythe compiles and delivers reports at minimum fill. <em>(V2)</em></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Dispute Settlement</strong></td><td>The decentralized arbitration process handled by the Tythe DAO Justice Arm. Participants stake TCT to raise a dispute. AI Clerks prepare evidence briefs from on-chain data. A human jury drawn from the disputant's TCT band delivers the verdict. Stake is burned if the slash is upheld, returned in full if overturned.</td></tr></tbody></table>
