#### 1. The Services

**1.1 The Tythe Protocol** The Tythe Protocol is a set of open-source smart contracts deployed on the Base blockchain and other compatible networks. The Protocol operates autonomously and is distinct from the Services operated by Tythe Labs, Inc. Tythe Labs does not control or operate the Protocol's smart contracts once deployed. By using the Services, you understand that you are interacting with self-executing smart contracts and that Tythe Labs does not intermediate, broker, or control any transactions executed through the Protocol.

**1.2 The Tythe Dashboard** The Tythe Dashboard at app.tythe.network provides a web-based interface for interacting with the Tythe Protocol. This includes creating and managing your Credit Enhancement Profile (CEP), viewing and refreshing your Tokenized Creditworthiness (TCT) score, accessing Credit Enhancement Vaults (CEV), participating in the Credit Data Exchange (CDX), and interacting with other Protocol modules.

**1.3 Credit Enhancement Profile (CEP)** The CEP is a self-sovereign financial identity anchored on the did:cheqd network. Creating a CEP requires completing identity verification and sanctions screening via Billions (ZKID Labs AG), our third-party zero-knowledge KYC provider. Your relationship with Billions is governed by Billions' own terms of service and privacy policy. Tythe receives only a cryptographic attestation confirming your verification status and does not receive, store, or process your raw identity documents or personal information.

Your CEP and its associated decentralized identifier (DID) are yours. Tythe cannot modify or revoke your CEP except as expressly permitted under this Agreement or required by applicable law. Tythe may freeze or flag your CEP if your compliance status is revoked or if you are found to have engaged in conduct that violates this Agreement or applicable law.

**1.4 Tokenized Creditworthiness (TCT)** TCT is a non-transferable ERC-20 token representing your creditworthiness as assessed by the Tythe Protocol's deterministic scoring formula. TCT scores are computed from publicly available on-chain behavioral data associated with your linked wallet addresses.

TCT is not a credit score as defined under the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) or any other consumer credit regulation. TCT is not a securities product, investment product, or financial instrument. TCT does not constitute a recommendation, guarantee, or representation regarding your creditworthiness for any purpose outside of the Tythe Protocol. Integrated markets and institutions make their own independent decisions regarding capital terms based on TCT signals.

**1.5 Credit Enhancement Wrapper (CEW)** The CEW is a smart contract proxy that applies TCT-based credit logic to financial transactions on integrated markets. CEW operates autonomously at the smart contract level. Tythe does not control or intermediate individual CEW-enhanced transactions once deployed.

**1.6 Credit Enhancement Vaults (CEV)** CEVs are ERC-4626 smart contract vaults deployed permissionlessly by verified curators. Tythe does not operate, manage, or control individual CEVs. Curators are solely responsible for their vault parameters, float deployment decisions, and borrower eligibility configurations. Tythe Labs is not a party to any transaction between CEV LPs, curators, and borrowers. Participation as an LP or borrower in any CEV involves significant financial risk including the risk of total loss of deposited capital. You should not participate in any CEV without fully understanding the risks involved.

**1.7 Credit Data Exchange (CDX)** The CDX is a sovereign data marketplace facilitated by Tythe. Participation as a data seller is entirely voluntary and consent-gated. Each dataset order requires independent, explicit opt-in. Tythe facilitates the compilation and delivery of Tythe Credit Reports but does not own, sell, or retain participant data outside of active, participant-approved dataset orders. Buyers receive a one-time report delivery and no ongoing data access after delivery.

**1.8 Tythe DAO** The Tythe DAO governs certain Protocol parameters through the Justice Arm and Governance Arm. The Justice Arm provides a decentralized dispute resolution process for contested TCT slash events. The Governance Arm manages scoring parameters and Protocol upgrades via TYT token governance. Tythe Labs does not control Tythe DAO decisions once governance is activated. DAO decisions are made by token holders and jury participants acting independently.

**1.9 Agentic Credit** Agentic Credit allows verified AI agents to access Tythe's credit infrastructure through a co-signer accountability model. Co-signers who onboard agents via Ricardian contract are legally and protocol-accountable for their agent's behavior on the Protocol. Co-signers acknowledge that their CEP and TYT stake may be affected by their agent's conduct. Tythe makes no representations regarding the performance, reliability, or safety of any AI agent operating on the Protocol.

**1.10 Third-Party Services** The Services may integrate with or provide access to third-party services, protocols, and platforms not operated or controlled by Tythe, including but not limited to Billions, the did:cheqd network, Morpho, and other integrated lending markets.
