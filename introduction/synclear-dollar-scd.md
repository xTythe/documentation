# Synclear Dollar: SCD

Synclear enables the creation and redemption of _SCD_, a fully-backed, on-chain synthetic dollar. _SCD_ is backed by crypto assets held in delta-neutral hedged positions and liquid stablecoins, never by fiat reserves in a bank. It is permissionless to mint, redeem, hold, and trade, and it composes freely throughout DeFi.

_SCD_ itself does not earn yield. Its yield-bearing counterpart, _stSCD_, captures the return generated by the backing and is covered under [SCD Yield Vaults](/broken/pages/1cTP7HBb1V0ka83XzbHg).

#### Peg stability

{% hint style="info" %}
_SCD_ derives its peg stability structurally, not from a defended reserve. Every unit of backing is held in one of two stable states: a stablecoin-denominated position worth a dollar (deployed into yield strategies, not sitting idle), or a volatile asset paired with an equal and opposite short position that offsets its price movement. Either way, the dollar value of the backing stays stable as markets move.
{% endhint %}

When the backing is a volatile asset such as ETH, the protocol holds a short perpetual position of equal notional against it. If ETH rises, the spot gains and the short loses by roughly the same amount; if ETH falls, the reverse. The combined position holds its dollar value, so _SCD_ stays fully backed without depending on the direction of the market. When hedging is not the right choice (explained below), the backing is held as liquid stables instead and routed to stablecoin-native strategies. Volatile backing is never left unhedged.

This is what lets _SCD_ hold its peg in most conditions. Anyone can also arbitrage the peg directly: if _SCD_ trades below a dollar, buy it and redeem it through the protocol for a dollar of backing; if it trades above, mint fresh _SCD_ at a dollar and sell it. See [Stabilize SCD](https://app.gitbook.com/o/0aiW3e41N69QHIAXxWlx/s/DJA9njN2uMlkeZuQfMaD/~/edit/~/changes/14/scd-mechanics/stabilize-scd) for the instant 1:1 path and the redemption mechanics.

#### How minting works

Minting _SCD_ is a direct swap, open to everyone with no KYC and no whitelist. You deposit USDC or USDT and receive SCD 1:1, with no fees, no execution cost, and no slippage. You are depositing a stablecoin, so nothing needs hedging at mint and SCD is issued immediately.

What the protocol does with that backing is a separate, ongoing decision, not tied to your individual deposit. The asset-management layer routes stable backing between two homes:

1. Delta-neutral hedges, when funding is favorable and a netting venue has capacity. The protocol buys spot and opens a short of equal notional in one cross-margin account on a perpetual DEX, so the position stays neutral to price while capturing funding.
2. Stablecoin yield, when hedging is not favorable, meaning funding is poor or a venue's position limit is reached. The backing stays in liquid stablecoins, which the protocol can hold without limit.

Backing stays on-chain throughout, held either in the protocol's own contracts or in the protocol's own accounts on decentralized perpetual venues (the hedge legs). There is no centralized custodian and no centralized exchange counterparty.

This is what lets SCD scale safely. The protocol never forces a hedge onto a venue that cannot absorb it, so it never drives funding against itself to hold the peg. When hedging capacity runs out, backing rotates to liquid stablecoins, which it can hold without limit. SCD supply is therefore bounded by the depth of stable backing available across DeFi, not by the open interest of any single perpetual venue.

Stable backing held outside hedges does not sit idle. Beyond a minimum reserve kept for instant redemptions, it is routed into the highest-yielding available alternatives to delta-neutral hedging: lending into the protocol's own SCD Credit Markets and external lending markets, fixed-yield positions, protocol-owned liquidity, and short-term instruments such as tokenized T-bills. When perpetual funding is poor (the same condition that makes hedging unattractive), these stable-yield sources are typically where the best risk-adjusted return sits anyway, so rotating to them is not a fallback that gives up yield, it is the protocol following yield to wherever the market is paying it.

#### Where the yield comes from

_SCD_ backing generates revenue from several independent sources, paid out to _stSCD_ holders:

1. Interest from the protocol's own SCD Credit Markets and external lending markets.
2. Soft-liquidation fees and hard-liquidation bonuses through the Stability Pool.
3. Fees from the competitive delta-neutral funding market, plus a share of the funding those positions capture.
4. A share of all protocol fees, plus a baseline reserve that puts idle stablecoins to work across fixed-yield positions (Pendle PT, blue-chip markets), protocol-owned SCD/USDC liquidity, and short-term instruments such as tokenized T-bills.

Because these sources do not all depend on the same market condition, _SCD_ backing produces yield across bull, neutral, and bear regimes rather than only when funding is high. Full detail is in [Yield Mechanics](https://app.gitbook.com/o/0aiW3e41N69QHIAXxWlx/s/DJA9njN2uMlkeZuQfMaD/~/edit/~/changes/10/scd-yield-vaults/yield-mechanics).

#### Risks

Interacting with _SCD_ carries risks that differ from a fiat-backed stablecoin, including but not limited to:

1. Smart contract risk
2. Market and hedging risk
3. Venue and execution risk (perpetual DEXs)
4. Oracle risk
5. Liquidity risk

Synclear mitigates these through multi-venue diversification, position limits, circuit breakers, oracle redundancy, and a Surplus Reserve, but they cannot be completely eliminated. Please read the [Risk Disclosure](https://app.gitbook.com/o/0aiW3e41N69QHIAXxWlx/s/DJA9njN2uMlkeZuQfMaD/~/edit/~/changes/10/risk-disclosure) in full before depositing.
