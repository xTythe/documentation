# Synclear Dollar: SCD

Synclear enables the creation and redemption of _SCD_, a fully-backed, on-chain synthetic dollar. _SCD_ is backed by crypto assets held in delta-neutral hedged positions and liquid stablecoins, never by fiat reserves in a bank. It is permissionless to mint, redeem, hold, and trade, and it composes freely throughout DeFi.

_SCD_ itself does not earn yield. Its yield-bearing counterpart, _stSCD_, captures the return generated by the backing and is covered under [SCD Yield Vaults](https://claude.ai/yield-vaults/yield-mechanics.md).

#### Peg stability

{% hint style="info" %}
_SCD_ derives its peg stability structurally, not from a defended reserve. Every unit of backing is held in one of two stable states: a liquid stablecoin worth a dollar, or a volatile asset paired with an equal and opposite short position that offsets its price movement. Either way, the dollar value of the backing stays stable as markets move.
{% endhint %}

When the backing is a volatile asset such as ETH, the protocol holds a short perpetual position of equal notional against it. If ETH rises, the spot gains and the short loses by roughly the same amount; if ETH falls, the reverse. The combined position holds its dollar value, so _SCD_ stays fully backed without depending on the direction of the market. When hedging is not the right choice (explained below), the asset is converted to a liquid stablecoin instead. Volatile backing is never left unhedged.

This is what lets _SCD_ hold its peg in most conditions. Anyone can also arbitrage the peg directly: if _SCD_ trades below a dollar, buy it and redeem it through the protocol for a dollar of backing; if it trades above, mint fresh _SCD_ at a dollar and sell it. See [Peg Stability Module (PSM)](https://app.gitbook.com/o/0aiW3e41N69QHIAXxWlx/s/DJA9njN2uMlkeZuQfMaD/~/edit/~/changes/5/management-and-stability/peg-stability-module-psm) for the instant 1:1 path and the redemption mechanics.

#### How minting works

Minting _SCD_ is a direct swap, open to everyone with no KYC and no whitelist. You deposit an allowlisted asset and receive _SCD_ one-to-one, less the cost of executing the hedge or conversion. The protocol decides at the moment of minting how to handle the deposit:

1. You deposit an allowlisted asset (a stablecoin, blue-chip crypto, an LST, or tokenized gold).
2. **If the deposit is already a stablecoin**, _SCD_ is issued one-to-one immediately. Nothing needs hedging.
3. **If the deposit is volatile and hedging conditions are favorable**, the protocol opens a delta-neutral short of equal notional on a perpetual DEX in the same flow, then issues _SCD_. The hedge's execution cost is priced into what you receive.
4. **If hedging is not favorable** (funding rates are poor, or the protocol's position limit on that venue is reached), the deposit is converted to a liquid stablecoin instead, and _SCD_ is issued against that. No short is opened.
5. Backing remains on-chain in protocol contracts throughout. There is no centralized custodian and no exchange counterparty holding the assets.

Step 4 is the mechanism that keeps _SCD_ safe to scale. The protocol never forces a hedge onto a venue that cannot absorb it, so it never drives funding rates against itself to maintain the peg. When hedging capacity runs out, backing rotates to liquid stablecoins, which the protocol can hold without limit. _SCD_ supply is therefore bounded by the depth of stable backing available across DeFi, not by the open interest of any single perpetual venue.

Converted stablecoins do not sit idle. Beyond a minimum reserve held for instant redemptions, they are routed into the highest-yielding available alternatives to delta-neutral hedging: lending into the protocol's own SCD Credit Markets and external lending markets, fixed-yield positions, protocol-owned liquidity, and short-term instruments such as tokenized T-bills. When perpetual funding is poor (the same condition that makes hedging unattractive), these stable-yield sources are typically where the best risk-adjusted return sits anyway, so rotating to them is not a fallback that gives up yield, it is the protocol following yield to wherever the market is paying it.

#### Where the yield comes from

_SCD_ backing generates revenue from several independent sources, paid out to _stSCD_ holders:

1. Funding and basis from the delta-neutral positions.
2. Interest from the protocol's own SCD Credit Markets (via the IMM) and external lending markets.
3. Soft-liquidation fees and hard-liquidation bonuses through the Stability Pool.
4. A share of all protocol fees, plus a baseline reserve that puts idle stablecoins to work across fixed-yield positions (Pendle PT, blue-chip markets), protocol-owned SCD/USDC liquidity, and short-term instruments such as tokenized T-bills.

Because these sources do not all depend on the same market condition, _SCD_ backing produces yield across bull, neutral, and bear regimes rather than only when funding is high. Full detail is in Yield Mechanics.

#### Risks

Interacting with _SCD_ carries risks that differ from a fiat-backed stablecoin, including but not limited to:

1. Smart contract risk
2. Market and hedging risk
3. Venue and execution risk (perpetual DEXs)
4. Oracle risk
5. Liquidity risk

Synclear mitigates these through multi-venue diversification, position limits, circuit breakers, oracle redundancy, and a Surplus Reserve, but they cannot be eliminated.
