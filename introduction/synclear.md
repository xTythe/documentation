# Synclear

{% hint style="info" %}
The Synclear Dollar (SCD) is not the same as a fiat stablecoin such as USDC, USDT, or PYUSD. SCD is a synthetic dollar, in the same family as Ethena's USDe or Falcon's USDf, backed by conservative dollar-stable holdings: liquid stablecoins, short-term instruments such as tokenized treasury bills, on-chain lending, and bounded delta-neutral hedged positions.

This means the risks of holding SCD are inherently different from those of a fiat-backed stablecoin. Please read the Risk Disclosure section before depositing.
{% endhint %}

#### Overview

Synclear is an on-chain dollar finance protocol. It issues SCD (Synclear Dollar), a conservative synthetic dollar, runs the savings accounts and investment funds that earn on it, and underwrites loans to on-chain businesses through transparent credit markets.

You mint SCD 1:1 by swapping USDC or USDT, and redeem it the same way on demand. Its backing is held conservatively and kept fully reserved, protected by a protocol-owned reserve, so the dollar stays whole while the backing works. SCD does not earn on its own. sSCD is its permissionless saved form, which earns the yield the backing generates and composes freely across open DeFi.

Beyond the dollar's own yield, the Strategy Funds open higher-yield strategies such as the Options Derivatives Fund and the Cashflow Credit Fund, where you hold each strategy's return and risk directly. They are funded separately and ring-fenced from SCD's backing, so the dollar stays conservative no matter how any strategy performs. You buy into Strategy Funds with USDC or USDT, fresh or redeemed from SCD you already hold.

The credit markets are where on-chain businesses borrow against their own revenue rather than by posting collateral, underwritten on live cash-flow data and repaid through automated revenue capture. The Cashflow Credit Fund supplies the loans, so its investors carry that risk, not SCD.&#x20;

> Automated revenue capture contains default risk but cannot mitigate it completely.\
> See the [Credit Markets](https://app.gitbook.com/s/DJA9njN2uMlkeZuQfMaD/credit-markets) section for how capture and underwriting work.

Synclear builds natively only the strategies that are its own edge, the options and credit engines, and allocates to established protocols (like [Ethena](https://ethena.fi) and [Theo](https://theo.xyz)). Hedging runs on vetted venues under qualified custody, and credit is underwritten on real cash-flow data. Backing is verifiable on-chain throughout.

#### What you can do

* **Mint SCD.** Swap USDC or USDT for SCD, 1:1. Open to everyone, with no KYC, no fee, and no slippage.
* **Save for sSCD.** Deposit SCD into Open Savings to earn the yield its backing generates. sSCD is the permissionless savings form of SCD, composable across open DeFi, with no KYC. \
  KYC-verified users can also access Gated Savings, which adds bounded delta-neutral strategies such as the Synclear Blended Options DN, Ethena Crypto Funding DN, and Theo Gold Carry DN.
* **Invest in Strategy Funds.** Opt into the Options Derivatives Fund, the Cashflow Credit Fund, and others as they launch, holding each strategy's return and risk directly. For KYC-verified users.
* **Borrow in the Credit Markets.** On-chain businesses borrow against their own captured revenue, underwritten on live cash-flow data and repaid transparently. For KYC-verified businesses.
* **Trade SCD anywhere.** Acquire or exit SCD freely on external pools such as Uniswap or Curve.
* **Redeem SCD.** Swap SCD back to USDC or USDT, 1:1, on demand.
* **Keep SCD at one dollar.** Anyone can arbitrage the peg by redeeming SCD when it trades below a dollar and minting when it trades above. See Stabilize SCD for how peg arbitrage works.

#### Access

Minting, holding, trading, and redeeming SCD are open to everyone, everywhere, with no KYC.

Open Savings is available to everyone with no KYC and no jurisdictional restriction. It holds only conservative dollar-stable positions: tokenized treasury bills, on-chain lending, and liquid stablecoins for cash. It is the one savings account open to all users.

Everything beyond Open Savings is for KYC-verified users and subject to the rules of your jurisdiction: Gated Savings, the Strategy Funds, and borrowing in the Credit Markets. Users are responsible for compliance with the laws that apply to them.
